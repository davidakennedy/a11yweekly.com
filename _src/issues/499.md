## Featured: AI doesn't fix accessible systems. It depends on them.

"For the last few years, our industry has been told a story: AI will personalize its way out of accessibility, deterministic design is dead, diagnosis can replace systems thinking, and structural work no longer needs to be done. None of it is true. AI cannot repair a broken structure. Instead, AI inherits it and reproduces it at scale."

Read more of [AI doesn't fix accessible systems. It depends on them.](https://annaecook.com/writing/2026/ai-doesnt-fix-accessible-systems-it-depends-on-them).

## Sponsored: "Your accessibility issues shouldn't break every time the page content changes

If you've used a testing tool that tracks issues by CSS selectors built on nth-child chains, you know the problem. A client adds a new product to a listing page, and suddenly half your tracked issues detach because the selectors no longer match. AAArdvark just shipped a fingerprinting approach to element matching. Instead of relying on brittle positional selectors, we look at the actual content of the element - image sources, text, CSS classes, link destinations, and more. Issues stay attached to the right element even when the page around them changes. If you've ever re-audited a page just to re-find issues you already logged, this is the fix.
